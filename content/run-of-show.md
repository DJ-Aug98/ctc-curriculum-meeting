# Run of Show — CTC Director Meeting
**Audience:** CTC Director
**Duration:** 60 minutes
**Facilitators:** CCIU team (2 recommended — one lead, one dashboard driver)
**Format:** Facilitated conversation anchored by interactive dashboard

---

## Pre-Meeting Setup
- Dashboard loaded and tested on display device
- 2–3 curriculum artifacts from SREB audit printed or queued on screen (lesson plans or curriculum maps)
- Sample unit plan template printed or queued
- Agenda leave-behind printed (one copy for director)

---

## Segment 1 — Welcome and Agenda Set
**Time:** 0:00–0:03 (3 min)
**Lead:** Primary facilitator

### Purpose
Set expectations, signal preparation, establish that this is a purposeful 60 minutes.

### Talk Track
Frame the hour around three outcomes:
1. Ground in what the SREB audit and focus groups told us
2. Share a framework and direction for addressing it
3. Leave with a clear next step

### Facilitation Notes
- Hand him a printed agenda or surface the agenda view on the dashboard
- Keep this tight — he'll know you're organized within the first 90 seconds
- Tone: unhurried, confident

---

## Segment 2 — Grounding: What We Know
**Time:** 0:03–0:13 (10 min)
**Lead:** Primary facilitator; second voice optional for focus group findings

### Purpose
Replace speculative discovery with evidence-based framing. You're not asking him to diagnose — you're showing what was found and taking his temperature.

### Content
- **SREB audit themes (pick 2–3 most salient):**
  - Inconsistency in instructional practice and curriculum documentation across three campuses
  - Curriculum living in teachers' heads, individual drives, or binders — no shared system
  - Misalignment to industry competencies and credential frameworks
- **Curriculum artifact display:** Place one example from the audit on the table or screen — a lesson plan or curriculum map that illustrates the current state. Do not editorialize. Let it sit for a moment.
- **Focus group finding (state explicitly):** *"One thing that came through clearly in focus groups is that newer teachers don't have a shared mental model of what a unit is versus a lesson — they're often working at the lesson level without a larger arc organizing their instruction."*

### Discovery Question
*"Of what we've found, what resonates most with what you're seeing from your seat?"*

- This is bounded, not open-ended
- One question only
- Listen for: where his energy sits — a specific campus, teacher capacity, credential outcomes, building trades

### Props
- Curriculum artifact from audit (printed or on screen)
- Dashboard: Audit findings panel

---

## Segment 3 — The Bridge
**Time:** 0:13–0:18 (5 min)
**Lead:** Primary facilitator

### Purpose
Connect what the audit found to what you're proposing. Short pivot — not a full argument.

### Talk Track
- Mirror back what he said in 1–2 sentences
- Name the two-part response: *"What we're proposing addresses this at two levels — the infrastructure level, meaning where curriculum lives and how it's organized, and the process level, meaning how teachers actually build it and the learning they need to do it well."*
- Parallel district note: *"We're actively building this with [district] right now, which means we're not theorizing — we're refining this in practice."*

### Facilitation Notes
- Keep this under 5 minutes — it's a pivot, not a section
- The parallel district mention is one sentence, not a case study
- Dashboard: transition to Two Pillars overview panel

---

## Segment 4 — The Framework: Two Pillars
**Time:** 0:18–0:38 (20 min)
**Lead:** Split between facilitators if possible — one pillar each

### Purpose
Present the core framework. Each pillar is framed as a response to a specific problem the audit and focus groups identified.

---

### Pillar 1 — Curriculum Infrastructure (8 min)

**Problem it solves:**
Curriculum lives in binders, individual drives, or nowhere — no shared visibility, no consistency across three campuses, no way for leadership to see what's being taught.

**What you're proposing:**
A structured, accessible home for curriculum — templated, mapped to competencies, visible to leadership across campuses, designed as a living document rather than a one-time artifact.

**Options (present the spectrum):**
See `optionality.md` for full detail on each option.
- Option A: Low-tech (Google Drive / Microsoft 365)
- Option B: Mid-tech (Notion / Airtable)
- Option C: Purpose-built platform (Atlas, Chalk, Toddle, Curriculum Trak)

**Non-negotiable across all options:**
The template structure and competency alignment logic stays consistent regardless of where it lives.

**State your recommendation and rationale** — but frame the decision as his.

---

### Pillar 2 — Writing Protocol + Teacher Learning (12 min)

**Problem it solves:**
Even with a home for curriculum, teachers need a shared process — and given the focus group findings, they need a conceptual foundation before the process will work. The professional learning design is load-bearing, not optional.

**The professional learning arc (name this explicitly):**
1. Foundational session — unit/lesson conceptual framework, exemplar review (~2–3 hours)
2. DACUM-style task analysis — surface competency clusters that become unit anchors
3. Collaborative unit writing — teachers draft using the protocol with facilitation support
4. Housing the output — finished units placed into the infrastructure

**Options (present the spectrum):**
See `optionality.md` for full detail on each option.
- Option A: DACUM-primary
- Option B: DACUM/UbD hybrid (recommended)
- Option C: UbD-primary with CTE adaptations

**Show the sample unit plan template here:**
Walk through it briefly — this is the picture of what the finished output looks like. For a Building Trades example if appropriate.

**State your recommendation:**
*"The hybrid is our recommended approach for a CTC context — it meets teachers where they are and builds toward something pedagogically coherent."*

### Props
- Dashboard: Pillar 1 options panel with interactive spectrum
- Dashboard: Pillar 2 options panel with interactive spectrum
- Sample unit plan template (printed or on screen)

---

## Segment 5 — Reaction and Discussion
**Time:** 0:38–0:48 (10 min)
**Lead:** Primary facilitator holds the space; both present

### Purpose
Temperature check. You've done most of the talking — give him room. Surface concerns before they become objections.

### Opening Prompt
*"What's landing? What questions does this raise?"*

### Anticipated Objections and Responses

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "Teachers are already stretched" | The PL arc is designed to happen in the context of doing real work — not in addition to it. Teachers leave a session with actual curriculum, not just training. |
| "What does this cost?" | Have rough platform cost ranges ready. Process cost is CCIU team time. Offer to follow up with a more detailed breakdown. |
| "How long does this take?" | A pilot through one program can produce finished unit plans within a semester if structured well. |
| "We already have curriculum somewhere" | Acknowledge it — and reference the audit artifacts as evidence of what "somewhere" looks like in practice. |
| "Will teachers actually use this?" | The PL arc is designed so teachers build the curriculum themselves — it's theirs, not something handed down. |

---

## Segment 6 — Optionality Decisions
**Time:** 0:48–0:55 (7 min)
**Lead:** Primary facilitator

### Purpose
Bring the conversation to a point of directional decision without forcing full resolution.

### Frame
*"We're not asking you to decide everything today — but it would help us to know where your head is on two things."*

### Question 1 — Infrastructure appetite
Is this a low-tech start or is there openness to a platform investment?

### Question 2 — Pilot starting point
Which program and which campus feels like the right starting point?
- Have Building Trades ready as an option — do not lead with it
- Let him name it if it's on his mind

### Goal
Leave with at least one directional answer, even if informal. This becomes the anchor for the next steps.

---

## Segment 7 — Next Steps and Close
**Time:** 0:55–1:00 (5 min)
**Lead:** Primary facilitator

### Proposed Next Step
A working session with 2–3 teachers to run a short DACUM-style task analysis as a proof of concept.

Frame as low-stakes: *"This gives you something real to react to before any larger commitment is made — and it gives your teachers a first experience of the process."*

### Confirm
- Who owns what between now and then
- Timeline for the working session
- Any information needed from his side (teacher availability, program priority confirmation)

### Leave-Behinds
- Printed agenda (already given at start)
- Sample unit plan template
- Infrastructure options one-pager
- Proposed agenda for the teacher working session

### Tone
Clean, decisive close. No lingering.
