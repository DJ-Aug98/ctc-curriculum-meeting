**Session Title:** [Program Area] Curriculum Working Session — Pilot
**Audience:** 2–3 [Program Area] teachers

**Duration:** 2.5–3 hours
**Facilitated by:** CCIU team

---

## Purpose

This session is the first concrete step in building curriculum infrastructure for CTC. Teachers leave with two real outputs — not training materials, not a framework overview, but actual curriculum work product:

1. A task inventory for [Program Area] — a validated list of what a competent practitioner in this field does, organized by duty area
2. A draft unit plan anchored to one of those duty areas — using the template structure shared in the director meeting

---

## Agenda

### Part 1 — Framing (20 min)
- Welcome and context: what this session is for and what it produces
- Brief orientation to the unit/lesson distinction — the conceptual foundation teachers need before the work begins
- Preview the unit plan template: what finished curriculum looks like in this model

### Part 2 — Task Analysis (60 min)
*DACUM-style, facilitated*
- Teachers surface and validate the tasks a competent [Program Area] practitioner performs
- Facilitator organizes tasks into duty areas in real time
- Group confirms groupings and identifies natural unit boundaries
- Output: a working task inventory organized by duty area

*Break — 10 min*

### Part 3 — Unit Draft (50 min)
*Collaborative, facilitator-supported*
- Group selects one duty area as the anchor for a first unit
- Facilitators walk through Stage 1 together: competency cluster, enduring understanding, essential questions, knowledge and skills
- Teachers draft with facilitator support — not lecture, not training
- Output: a partially completed Stage 1 for one unit

### Part 4 — Close and Next Steps (20 min)
- Debrief: what worked, what needs refinement
- Name what comes next: remaining units, Stages 2 and 3, housing in infrastructure
- Confirm timeline and ownership for completing the unit plan
- Preview what a full curriculum map for [Program Area] would look like once all units are drafted

## Outputs from This Session
