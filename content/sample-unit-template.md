# Sample Unit Plan Template — Outline and Field Descriptions

This template is the tangible artifact of the writing protocol. It should be brought to the director meeting as a prop — either printed or displayed — to make the "finished product" concrete. The example is kept content-agnostic for now — program-specific versions (e.g., Building Trades) can be toggled in once a pilot program is confirmed.

The template reflects the DACUM/UbD hybrid approach (Option B in `optionality.md`) but can be adapted for DACUM-primary or UbD-primary variations.

### Stage 1 — Desired Results *(UbD-informed)*

**Competency Cluster**
The duty area and associated tasks from the DACUM task inventory that this unit addresses.
*Example: Duty Area C — Framing / Tasks C1–C7*

**Industry Credential Alignment**
Which credential, certification, or standard this unit supports.
*Example: NOCTI Building Construction, Domain 3*

**Enduring Understanding**
The transferable insight students will carry beyond this unit — the "so what" of the competency cluster.
*Example: "Structural integrity depends on precision at every stage — errors in framing compound through every subsequent phase of construction."*

**Essential Questions**
2–3 open questions that drive inquiry throughout the unit. These are questions students should still be wrestling with at the end, not questions with a single correct answer.
*Example:*
- *"How does a mistake in rough framing affect the finished structure?"*
- *"What does a professional carpenter check before moving to the next phase, and why?"*

**Knowledge and Skills**
What students will know (declarative knowledge) and be able to do (procedural skill) by the end of the unit.

*Students will know:*
- [List key concepts, terminology, safety requirements]

*Students will be able to:*
- [List observable, measurable competencies — drawn directly from task inventory]

### Stage 2 — Assessment Evidence *(UbD-informed)*

**Performance Task**
The authentic, culminating assessment that demonstrates mastery of the unit's competency cluster. Should mirror real-world work where possible.
*Example: "Students will frame a 10x12 shed structure to code, demonstrating accurate layout, plumb and level installation, and proper fastening technique. Work will be evaluated against a trade-standard checklist."*

**Assessment Criteria / Rubric Reference**
How the performance task will be evaluated. Reference the rubric or checklist used.

**Other Evidence**
Supporting assessments used throughout the unit (formative checks, quizzes, observation logs, safety certifications, etc.)

## Notes for Meeting Use

### As a prop
- Print one completed example (even if partially filled) to place on the table during Pillar 2
- Keep content-agnostic until pilot program is confirmed — swap in program-specific content as a toggle once decided
- The goal is not to analyze the template in the meeting — it is to make the finished product tangible

### What the template demonstrates
- The unit/lesson distinction is built into the structure — Stage 3 contains the lesson sequence, Stages 1 and 2 define what the lessons are building toward
- Competency alignment is explicit and traceable — the DACUM task inventory connects directly to Stage 1
- The template works regardless of which infrastructure option is chosen — it is a document structure, not a platform feature

### Anticipated questions
- *"This seems like a lot of paperwork"* → The template is a thinking tool, not a filing requirement. Teachers who understand the stages internalize the logic and don't experience it as overhead.
- *"Do teachers fill this out alone?"* → No — the writing protocol is collaborative. Units are drafted in facilitated sessions with peer review built in.
- *"What does a completed one actually look like?"* → This is why you bring the exemplar.
