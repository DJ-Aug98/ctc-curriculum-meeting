# Curriculum Infrastructure — Options Overview
*Leave-behind for CTC Director Meeting*

---

## The Decision
Where does curriculum live — and how is it organized so it's visible, consistent, and sustainable across all three campuses?

The template structure, competency alignment logic, and naming conventions are defined independent of the platform. They carry over regardless of which option is chosen or when you migrate.

---

## Option A — Low-Tech
**Google Drive / Microsoft 365**

Structured folder architecture, standardized naming conventions, shared template files, and a master alignment tracker in a spreadsheet.

| Pros | Cons |
|---|---|
| Zero additional cost | Degrades over time without active maintenance |
| No learning curve — tools teachers already use | No built-in alignment reporting or cross-campus visibility |
| Immediately deployable | Hard to search across programs at scale |
| | Version control is manual |

**Best fit:** Budget is constrained, or you want to prove the curriculum model before investing in a platform.

---

## Option B — Mid-Tech
**Notion / Airtable or similar**

A relational workspace where units, competencies, and assessments are linked — not just filed. Dashboards give leadership a navigable view across programs and campuses.

| Pros | Cons |
|---|---|
| Low cost (free tiers available) | Requires someone to design and maintain the workspace |
| Relational structure enables cross-referencing | Not purpose-built for curriculum — some workarounds needed |
| Flexible as the model evolves | Adoption requires onboarding |
| Visually navigable for teachers and leaders | |

**Best fit:** Some internal tech-forward capacity exists; you want more structural power without a full platform commitment.

---

## Option C — Purpose-Built Platform
**Atlas, Chalk, Toddle, or Curriculum Trak**

Dedicated curriculum mapping software — built-in competency alignment, cross-campus visibility, teacher-facing authoring, leadership reporting.

| Platform | Notable Strength |
|---|---|
| Atlas (Rubicon) | Strong UbD alignment, widely used in K-12, good cross-campus reporting |
| Chalk | Clean teacher UI, strong CTE competency mapping |
| Toddle | Collaborative unit planning features |
| Curriculum Trak | Purpose-built for CTE, direct competency alignment |

| Pros | Cons |
|---|---|
| Highest long-term ceiling | Real cost — per-seat or institutional licensing |
| Most sustainable without internal maintenance | Requires onboarding and adoption effort |
| Reporting and alignment built in | Can be overkill if the writing process isn't solid first |

**Best fit:** Ready for a platform investment; long-term sustainability and leadership reporting are priorities.

---

## Our Recommendation

**Start with Option A or B** — whichever matches your current platform appetite — with a clear intention to migrate to Option C once the curriculum writing process is established and the model is proven.

Starting with a platform before the process is solid risks an expensive empty shell. Build the process first. The platform serves it, not the other way around.
